MANIFESTO

THIS IS A PUBLIC
SERVICE ANNOUNCEMENT

By riding with SUGO you agree to this Manifesto.
Read it. Mean it. Ride accordingly.

SHOULD I STAY
OR SHOULD I GO

SUGO exists because we didn’t fit elsewhere.
It’s not for everyone, and it doesn’t try to be.
If this doesn’t feel right, there are other roads.

NO DISCRIMINATION,
NO APOLOGIES

SUGO has zero tolerance for discrimination — full stop.
On the bike, though, we care about pace,
control, and awareness.
We ride fast. Mostly road, some gravel.
This is not a training ground.

MOSTLY TOGETHER,
ALWAYS CONNECTED

We usually move as one.
If one gets dropped, we wait at the top.
Some rides are drop rides — that’s part of the deal,
but it will be announced upfront.
You might be dropped one day, but it won't be forever.

NO SPONSORS,
NO HANDOUTS

SUGO runs on its own legs.
We do some partnerships, but we cannot guarantee anything.
If you think the cost is too high, you’re missing the point.
If you have ideas, we are all ears.

FAST, NOT STUPID

Speed is fun. Carelessness is not.
Helmet on. Clean lines.
Lights at night. Take care and brush your hair.

KNOW WHEN TO PULL,
KNOW WHEN TO SUFFER

No egos, no heroes.
Hold the wheel or let it go.
Choose the right ride for your legs.
If you’re here, you’re expected to ride like it.

SHOW UP OR SHUT UP

SUGO is built by riding, not talking.
Be present. Be consistent.

NO DESTINATION,
FULL GAS

We don’t know where this is going.
Long rides. Fast days. Bad ideas. Good coffee.
That’s enough.
